Google Tilt Brush

Tilt Brush is a room-scale 3D-painting virtual-reality application available from Google. Tilt Brush lets you paint in 3D space with virtual reality goggles. Your room is your canvas. Your palette is your imagination. The possibilities are endless.

Star Surfer - credit: Kay Tennemann

How do I use Google Tilt Brush?

Users are presented with a virtual palette hovering above the hand controller from which they can select from a variety of brush types, colors, effects, and tools to paint their virtual creations. Movement of the handheld controller, using an intuitive interface in 3D space, creates brush strokes that follow in the virtual environment. With Dynamic Brushes ranging from ink and smoke to snow and fire, you can explore the wonders of a virtual palette. You can also capture snapshots, animated GIF images, .mpeg videos, or render 360 degree videos.

How much does the Tilt Brush cost?

The Oculus version of Tilt Brush is $30, and the Vive HTC version is similiar. Google has released a free set of tools to help developers transform Tilt Brush sketches into immersive 3D video animations. The company recently released the Tilt Brush 3D sketch program and is testing the tech with dozens of established artists via its 'Tilt Artists in Residence' program.

How was the Tilt Brush developed?

Tilt Brush was developed by Skillman & Hackett in 2014. It was nominated for four Proto Awards (namely: "Best Interaction Design", "Most Innovative", "Best Overall Virtual Reality Application", and "Best GUI"), of which it won the "Best GUI" award. Google acquired Tilt Brush in 2015. Tilt Brush was released for the HTC Vive virtual reality goggles at its launch on April 5, 2016, at no cost when pre-ordering the HTC Vive. On February 24, 2017, Tilt VR announced it is now available on both Oculus Rift and Vive. In a Fast Company article about Tilt Brush, one of the creators said that the idea of drawing in 3D space came from a chess game prototype: There was a happy accident. Tilt Brush came out of an experiment with a virtual reality chess prototype, where we accidentally started painting the chess pieces in the air, and it was incredible!.

Reception by the Media

Ars Technica and other sites referred to Tilt Brush as the HTC Vive's killer app, praising the program's intuitive interface and the excitement of painting in three dimensions. Edward Baig of USA Today said that the program was the only one that excited him in the platform's launch lineup. Chris Suellentrop of The New York Times wrote that the program was well suited for beginners. In 2018, Tilt Brush was employed for the first time in mixed reality news reporting by the NBC News studio 'NBC Left Field'.

Awards

Tilt Brush has earned numerous technology and innovation awards.

  • Webby Award 2018: Best of VR: Interactive, Game or Real-Time
  • Cannes Lions 2017: 2 Gold Lions (Innovation, Digital Craft), 1 Silver
  • Lumiere Award for Best VR Experience 2017
  • Cinequest 2017 Best Interactive Experience
  • CES 2017 Best of Innovations: Virtual Reality
  • SXSW 2017 Interactive Innovation Award for VR/AR

Above are the top six most recent awards. There will certainly be more awards as new versions with upgraded features are published in the future.
